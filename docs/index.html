Write up

This IK (Inverse Kinematics) Simulation Project is an interactive 3D application developed in Processing. The simulation includes features like joint limits, obstacle collision detection, user-interaction, moving IK, and 3D rendering&camera.

I encountered several difficulties during the development, such as applying obstacle collision detection to the system simulation. I have spent too much time and tried a lot of different methods to pervent the joints getting stuck in the obstacle, but none of them worked as perfectly as I expected. At the end, I settled with the methods from project 1. I have also tried to implement 3D render for the skeleton and Re-rooting IK for the system, but both failed after getting no progress in solving the angle issue in processing.

Camera Controls

- Movement: Use 'q', 'w', 'e', 'a', 's', 'd' to navigate the camera location.
- Speed up: Hold 'shift' to boost the move speed while using movement keys to raise or lower the camera's position.
- Angle: Use the arrow keys 'up', 'down', 'left', 'right' to adjust the camera orientation.

Simulation Acceleration Cap Controls

- Apply/Cancel acceleration cap: Press 'n' to activate or deactivate acceleration cap in the movement of the IK systems.

Skeleton Simulation Controls

- Pause/Resume: Press 'Space' to toggle between pausing and resuming the simulation.
- Reset: Hit 'r' to reset the simulation to its initial state.
- Left/Right Arn Switch: Press 't' to toggle the movement control between the left and right arm of the IK system.
- Walking/Moving root: Press 'k' to initiate movement of the entire IK skeleton.
- Cancel joint limit: Press 'l' to reinstate joint limits, allowing for unrestricted movement of the joints
- Camera Info: Press 'p' to print out the current camera location information.
